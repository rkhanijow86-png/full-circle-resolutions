The Wendon Project

A homegrown wellness coalition for West Cork.

The Wendon Project is a service-oriented initiative built for the place I live, gathering local practitioners, sound healers, and facilitators to give back to the community that has held me.

Three focus areas

How the Wendon Project shows up in West Cork.

01

Creative & Wellness Coalition

Local hands. Local healing.

A growing network of West Cork practitioners, therapists, yoga teachers, artists, mediators, somatic workers, who gather under the Wendon Project to offer their skills to people in the community who couldn't otherwise access them.

We organise pop-up wellness days, sliding-scale clinics, and skills-share evenings. If you're a practitioner who wants to be part of it, please get in touch.

Join the coalition →
02

Sound-Based Therapies

Resonance as medicine.

Sound work, bowls, gongs, voice, ambient acoustics, is one of the oldest tools we have for shifting the state of a room or a nervous system. I offer regular community sound baths and bespoke sessions for individuals, groups, and post-circle integration.

  • Open community sound baths (donation-based)
  • Private sessions for individuals or couples
  • Sound integration following mediation or restorative circles
  • Workshops introducing the practice to other facilitators
Enquire about a session →
03

Specialised Facilitation

For the conversations no one wants to lead.

Some topics ask for a facilitator who has done the inner work to hold them well. The Wendon Project leads programming around domestic violence, sexual assault awareness, post-incident community response, and other trauma-informed initiatives.

This work is offered to schools, community groups, and local government, on a sliding scale or pro bono where need outpaces budget.

  • Sexual Assault Awareness Month programming
  • Trauma-informed staff training
  • Schools-based restorative practice
  • Community response after public harm
