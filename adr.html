Alternative Dispute Resolution

Conflict resolution that remembers the people inside the dispute.

My ADR practice combines the structure of formal mediation with the warmth of a wellness-led environment. The legal craft is rigorous. The space around it is deeply human.

ADR & mediation

A way through, that doesn't require a courtroom.

Alternative Dispute Resolution, mediation, arbitration, restorative practice, resolves conflict outside of formal litigation. Done well, it is faster, more private, less costly, and far better at preserving relationships than adversarial routes. As a mediator I don't decide outcomes. I hold the structure of the conversation, pace, safety, fairness, so the people closest to the dispute can hear each other and craft an agreement that actually works in their lives.

Who this serves

Workplace conflict · Co-founder disputes · Family & relational repair · Community grievances · Post-incident facilitation · Cross-cultural dialogue · Restorative practice in schools

Sub-practice 01

Holistic Dispute Resolution

Conflict resolution isn't just about legalities; it's about the people involved. Offered as session-based mediation in a calm, considered setting, or as a multi-day retreat where care is woven into every moment.

Two formats

Choose the shape that fits the dispute.

A Mediation Hourly or day-rate · In person or online

Session-based mediation

Structured mediation offered as standalone sessions, anywhere from a focused two hours to a full day, in person at my West Cork practice or online. The right fit when the situation is contained, the parties want to keep moving, or a retreat isn't practical.

The legal craft is rigorous: a clear container, balanced airtime, a documented agreement at the end where useful. The atmosphere is warm rather than clinical, but the work is precise.

  • i

    Joint or shuttle sessions

    We meet together, or I move between parties, whichever serves safety and progress best.

  • ii

    Pre-mediation intake

    Each party has a private conversation with me first, so the session itself can be used well.

  • iii

    Documented outcomes

    Where it serves, the session ends with a written summary or signed agreement.

  • iv

    Confidential throughout

    Without exception. What is said in the session stays in the session.

B Retreats Three to five days · Held on the West Cork coast

Multi-day holistic retreats

A retreat is structured like a held breath, then a release. Three to five days in spaces chosen for their warmth and quiet, moving between formal mediation sessions and the practices that make those sessions possible: movement, nourishment, time outdoors, time alone.

  • i

    Integration

    Yoga, sound therapy, breathwork, and grounding time in nature woven through the formal mediation schedule.

  • ii

    Holistic Mediation

    Formal mediation held in a setting designed to support the nervous system. The legal craft is rigorous; the space around it is somatic, relational, and unhurried.

  • iii

    Wellness Breaks

    Movement, sound, and time outdoors woven between sessions. The breaks are not asides, they are part of the work.

  • iv

    Ceremonial Resolution

    When the work is complete, we close with intention, a ritual that marks the agreement, honours the difficulty, and helps everyone leave whole.

  • v

    Trauma-Informed Pacing

    Sessions are designed with the nervous system in mind. Breaks, soft transitions, and consent are built into the structure.

Sub-practice 02

Restorative Circles

Facilitated circles designed to address community or individual harm within professional settings. Where the issue is collective, the response must be too.

Applications

For organisations, schools, agencies, and the communities they serve.

A restorative circle is a structured group conversation, every voice held, no voice rushed. It's the right tool when something has happened that affects more than two people, or when a culture has frayed and needs to be re-stitched in the open.

Role of the facilitator

A circle is only as honest as the person holding it.

My role is to set the structure, agreements, pace, who speaks when, and then to recede. Good facilitation feels almost invisible: it makes hard truth feel safer to say, and silence feel productive rather than awkward. I bring a trauma-informed, wellness-oriented presence, tracking the room's nervous system, not just its words.

  • i

    Resolving organisational conflict

    Teams, leadership, board-level dynamics. When the spreadsheet says everything is fine and the room says it isn't.

  • ii

    Addressing community-wide grievances

    Neighbourhood disputes, post-incident response, hearings where the community needs to feel met, not managed.

  • iii

    Facilitating dialogue after individual harm

    One-on-one or small-group circles between those harmed and those responsible, voluntary, prepared, and held with care.

  • iv

    Restorative practice in schools

    Working with educators and pupils to embed a culture of repair over punishment.

How a circle unfolds

01

Listening intake

I meet with each participant one-on-one before the circle. No agenda. Just a sense of what's true for each person.

02

Designing the container

Together we shape the agreements, the questions, the rhythm, what this particular circle needs to be safe and useful.

03

Holding the circle

The session itself, anywhere from two hours to a full day. Talking pieces, equal time, careful listening.

04

Follow-through

Documenting agreements, scheduling check-ins, supporting the work that lives between sessions.
